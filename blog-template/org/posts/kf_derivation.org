* First
Start with the linear first order ODE.

\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial \mathbf{x}(t)}{\partial t} = \mathbf{A} \mathbf{x}(t) + \mathbf{B} w
\end{equation}

To solve, let the integrating factor be $e^{At}$.  Multiply this to both sides:
\begin{equation}
e^{At} \frac{\partial \mathbf{x}(t)}{\partial t} - e^{At} \mathbf{A} \mathbf{x}(t)= e^{At}  \mathbf{B} w
\end{equation}
The product rule allows us to do this:
\begin{equation}
e^{At} \frac{\partial \mathbf{x}(t)}{\partial t} + e^{At} \mathbf{A} \mathbf{x}(t)=\frac{\partial}{\partial{t}} \mathbf{x}(t) e^{At}
\end{equation}
