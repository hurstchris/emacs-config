Introduction

The dot product is often introduced in two ways:

  1. Algebraic definition
  2. Geometric definition

The goal is to show that these definitions are equivalent.

Algebraic definition

Let

\[ \mathbf{a} = (a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n) \]

\[ \mathbf{b} = (b_1, b_2, \dots, b_n) \]

The dot product is defined as

\[ \mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i b_i \]

Geometric definition

The dot product can also be written as

\[ \mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b} = ||\mathbf{a}|| \, ||\mathbf{b}|| \cos(\theta) \]

where

  • \(||\mathbf{a}||\) is the magnitude of vector a
  • \(||\mathbf{b}||\) is the magnitude of vector b
  • \(\theta\) is the angle between them

Goal

Show that

\[ \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i b_i = ||\mathbf{a}|| \, ||\mathbf{b}|| \cos(\theta) \]

Proof idea

The proof relies on the law of cosines.

Consider the vector

\[ \mathbf{c} = \mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b} \]

Then

\[ (|| \mathbf{c}||) ^2 = (\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}) \cdot (\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}) \]

Expand the expression

Expanding gives

\[ (||\mathbf{a}||)^2 + (||\mathbf{b}||)^2 - 2(\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}) \]

Apply the law of cosines

From geometry,

\[ ||\mathbf{c}||^2 =||\mathbf{a}||^2 +||\mathbf{b}||^2-2 ||\mathbf{a}|| ||\mathbf{b}|| \cos(\theta) \]

Equate the two expressions

Comparing the expressions gives

\[ \mathbf{a} ⋅ \mathbf{b} =

  \mathbf{a}       \mathbf{b}   cos(θ)

\]

Interpretation

The dot product measures how aligned two vectors are.

Special cases:

  • \(\theta = 0\): vectors aligned → maximum dot product
  • \(\theta = \pi/2\): orthogonal → dot product = 0
  • \(\theta = \pi\): opposite directions → negative dot product

Summary

Two equivalent views of the dot product:

  1. Algebraic: sum of component-wise products
  2. Geometric: product of magnitudes scaled by cosine of angle

Both represent the same operation.
